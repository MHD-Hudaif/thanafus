CHAPTER 1: ORIGIN, PURPOSE, AND VISION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
001: The word "Thanafus" is derived from the classical Arabic word "Tanaafus",
002: which signifies noble rivalry and healthy competition in righteous deeds, 
003: knowledge, and virtuous character. The festival is designed to provide 
004: a vibrant platform for young islamic scholars to showcase their co-curricular 
005: talents.
006: 
007: Hosted and executed by Al Jamiathul Kauzariyya (kauzariyya.com) in Edathala, 
008: Aluva, Kerala, Thanafus is conducted annually (every single academic year). 
009: It represents a cornerstone of the institution's commitment to balanced 
010: personal growth, alongside rigorous theological study.
011: 
012: The festival is strictly an internal championship for the student body. 
013: It aims to build public speaking confidence, foster literary analysis, and 
014: encourage digital artistic expressions, helping candidates translate academic 
015: theories into active cultural communications.
016: 
017: Unity and respect among participating students remain the bedrock of the 
018: festival. Any competition style that compromises brotherhood or academic 
019: ethics is strictly prohibited.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHAPTER 2: DIVISION AND SECTIONS MAPPING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
020: To ensure fair evaluations, the student body of Al Jamiathul Kauzariyya, 
021: including the Hifz section and the Alumni sections, is partitioned into 
022: three distinct competition divisions.
023: 
024: The mapping of sections for Thanafus is defined as follows:
025: 
026: 1. SUBJUNIOR DIVISION:
027: - Composed entirely of students from the Hifz Section (memorization academy).
028: - Focuses on core recitation, memorization, basic speeches, and visual art.
029: 
030: 2. JUNIOR DIVISION:
031: - Composed of students from the Sanaviyya Subsection (Secondary/Alumni level).
032: - Evaluated on intermediate literature, multi-language speech, and quizzes.
033: 
034: 3. SENIOR DIVISION:
035: - Composed of students from the Aliya Subsection (Undergraduate and Post-Graduate Fiqh & Ifta level).
036: - Focuses on advanced legal debates, Tafseer research, and complex literature.
037: 
038: Every student is eligible to join programs designated for their division. 
039: Select programs are marked as "Open Category", permitting candidates from 
040: all three divisions to compete on the same stage.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHAPTER 3: THE TEAMING DRAFT SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
041: The Musabaqa is fought between four dedicated campus teams. The formation 
042: of these teams follows a strict draft system to ensure equal distribution 
043: of talent.
044: 
045: THE TEAM SELECTION PROCESS:
046: 
047: STEP 1: LEADER SELECTION
048: - First, four outstanding students from the final year (last year) class are 
049:   selected by the festival committee to serve as the four team leaders.
050: 
051: STEP 2: LEADER DECISIONS
052: - Each chosen leader is responsible for deciding their team's name and 
053:   official team color.
054: - No two teams can share the same color or theme.
055: 
056: STEP 3: THE DRAFT ROUNDS
057: - The draft is conducted in a turn-based manner.
058: - The four leaders take turns selecting one student at a time from the general 
059:   student pool.
060: - The draft order rotates (Leader 1, Leader 2, Leader 3, Leader 4, then Leader 4,
061:   Leader 3, Leader 2, Leader 1) to maintain structural fairness.
062: - Selection rounds continue until no student is left and all four teams are 
063:   fully and equally filled.
064: 
065: Once the draft is completed and published, team transfers are strictly barred 
066: unless approved by the Chief Patron under medical circumstances.

CHAPTER 4: COMPETITION VENUES AND STAGES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
067: All stage items run concurrently across four designated areas on campus:
068: 
069: 1. STAGE A: BAGHDAD (Main Stage)
070: - Located in the central open courtyard of Al Jamiathul Kauzariyya.
071: - Dedicated to grand inaugurals, Arabic and English public speaking, group 
072:   song recitals, and closing assemblies.
073: 
074: 2. STAGE B: CORDOBA
075: - Located in the main seminar hall.
076: - Dedicated to Urdu and Malayalam elocution, quiz competitions, Fiqh debates, 
077:   and digital presentation contests.
078: 
079: 3. STAGE C: MEDINA
080: - Located in the spacious mosque lobby.
081: - Reserved for Qur'an recitation, Hifz assessments, Azan evaluations, and 
082:   classical speech programs.
083: 
084: 4. STAGE D: DAMASCUS
085: - Located in the library annex rooms.
086: - Reserved for off-stage writing, Calligraphy, geometric illustration, and 
087:   wall magazine layouts.

CHAPTER 5: SCORING SYSTEM AND LEADERBOARD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
088: Points are allocated to determine both individual awards and the overall 
089: team championship leaderboard.
090: 
091: INDIVIDUAL EVENTS:
092: - First Place (A Grade): 10 Points
093: - Second Place (A Grade): 7 Points
094: - Third Place (A Grade): 5 Points
095: - A Grade (Non-placing): 3 Points
096: - B Grade: 1 Point
097: 
098: GROUP EVENTS:
099: - First Place: 15 Points
100: - Second Place: 10 Points
101: - Third Place: 7 Points
102: 
103: The team accumulating the highest total score across all divisions will be 
104: crowned the Thanafus Champions.
105: 
106: In case of a tie for the final championship, the team with the higher number 
107: of First Place finishes will be declared the winner.
108: 
109: The "Kalathilakam" trophy is awarded to the student securing the highest 
110: overall score in individual events.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHAPTER 6: GENERAL CODES AND CONDUCT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
111: Absolute discipline must be maintained by all teams. Contestants must 
112: display exemplary moral behavior.
113: 
114: Major violations that lead to immediate disqualification:
115: 1. Plagiarism in poetry, writing, and illustration submissions.
116: 2. Using unauthorized materials or internet devices during closed-room contests.
117: 3. Showing disrespect towards judges, stage coordinators, or fellow teams.
118: 4. Arriving late to the stage call by more than 10 minutes.
119: 
120: All complaints regarding evaluation must be filed in writing to the Appeals 
121: Committee within 1 hour of score publication.
122: 
123: The decision of the Appeals Committee is final and binding on all four teams.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHAPTER 7: PROGRAM RULES AND ELIGIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
124: 1. Contestants can register for a maximum of 3 individual events and 2 group 
125:    events to prevent schedule congestion.
126: 2. All registrations must be verified by the respective team leader.
127: 3. If a scheduling conflict occurs, the student must notify the Stage Marshall 
128:    at least 2 hours in advance to explore alternative slots.
129: 4. Evaluation sheets are kept confidential and are archived by the registrar.
130: 5. Time limits for speeches must be strictly followed (5 mins for Senior, 
131:    4 mins for Junior, 3 mins for Subjunior).
